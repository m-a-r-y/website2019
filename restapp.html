Food Ordering Form

Objective

The objective of this class project was to create a form where hungry students could quickly order food from an application without being overwhelmed by visiting dozens of different websites for restaurants and menu choices.

The Story

This was the first project that I built putting together all the technologies that was gained within the first week of lessons. The focus in the beginning was on pure functionality and less on design; the form below was constructed with pure HTML and CSS. The goal was to create a form with 4 restaurant choices each with 4 of their most basic menu items.

Form Version #1: Minimalistic

After completing the functionality of the restaurant app, the layout appeared plain so I added an image to the app to improve its appearance.

Form Version #2: With Visual

Challenges

The biggest challenge was to make the food ordering form more visually appealing to the users and to also make it mobile friendly and responsive.

After some research on color psychology and how it affect moods and emotions, I wanted to find colors that are associated with food and stimulate a healthy appetite.

Color Psychology (source)

From the color psychology chart above, I wanted to focus on a warm color to achieve the desire effects of inducing excitement and hunger in users. Thus, I decided on a orange-yellow background color to reference food and hunger along with a red submit button to encourage determination and quick actions. In addition, I also included Bootstrap to enhance the overall appearance of the app to give it a more modern look and making it mobile-friendly and responsive across different devices.

Takeaways

Through this project, I learned how to utilize Bootstrap to make a web app responsive on mobile for a better user experience regardless of what device the user is currently using. Also, color psychology and visuals are also important factors to consider when designing to make any application or website more aesthetically appealing.

To further improve upon this application, I would create a more attractive call-to-action button instead of a simple "SUBMIT" button; research have shown that creative CTAs are more effective in user engagement. In addition, instead of a dropdown restaurant list, I would lay out the restaurants options in two rows on the left and finally, I would redesign the application so that the form would be on the right of the restaurant display.

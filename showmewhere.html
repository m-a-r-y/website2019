Show Me Where

Objective

This project was created with several teammates for a 2014 Health 2.0 Code-a-thon. Within 24 hours, we had to utilize the BetterDoctor API to design and build a model application that best demonstrates the power of BetterDoctor - a database of best doctors and their information for your specific needs.

The Story

Show Me Where was created with Node.js and Ionic, an HTML5 hybrid mobile app framework. This application combines BetterDoctor API and UberX API to recommend a doctor based on the user's injury, and displays the time and cost that will take to get to your doctor via Uber.

Displays a map and a scroll down list of specialty doctors.

Challenges

One of the biggest challenge for this project was the constricted time frame and the collaboration of many people working on the project at once. With 6 people on the team, we had to agree upon a single product idea and quickly assign tasks and fairly delegate responsibilities. Our approach to this was to break into pairs; one pair worked on the backend and another worked on the frontend while the last pair jumped between the two to assist with extra help.

My partner and I were assigned with front-end development and creating pages for the app. Even between two people, there were minor conflicts on how to structure the code and how the design should look. Since time was of the essence, we decided to split the assignment and work independently on individual pages. Even though my partner and I worked separately on our own pages, I made sure to communicate to my partner and to everyone of what I was doing to make sure that our app had a uniformed look.

Takeaways

This Code-a-thon was an excellent exercise in collaboration and teamwork while on a tight deadline; effective communication and time management was crucial throughout the process. I also realized that different projects require different technologies and frameworks so it was a fun challenge to quickly pick up Ionic and use the framework for the first time just for this web app.

Tools Used

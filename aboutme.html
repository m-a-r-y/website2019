About me

Standing at the intersection of design, technology, and social sciences, and I am passionate about creating products that optimize the human experience and influence positive social change. How I got here

I received my Masters in Information Science with a focus on human-computer interactions from Cornell University. For my capstone projects, I worked with external clients as the Lead UX/UI Designer to solve their design problems. Additionally, I created avatars and virtual environments for a VR companionship study as a Research Assistant in the Virtual Embodiment Lab.

Prior to Cornell, I was a Front-end Developer and created websites and digital products. I became proficient with design tools such as Sketch, Balsamiq, InVision, and Adobe Photoshop; I also honed my front-end development skills and created mockups with HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

Before design and technology, I fostered my deep-rooted enthusiasm for social impact from my undergraduate degrees in International Studies and Sociology from UC Irvine.
